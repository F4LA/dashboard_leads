How to use this dashboard
The one rule: always start with volume
Before looking at any other metric, check Booked Calls. If you don't have enough people entering the funnel, nothing else matters. There's no point optimizing the sales call if only 20 people booked this month. Volume first, everything else second.
Weekly check-in (5 minutes)
Every week, open the dashboard and ask three questions:

1. Do I have ~16 booked calls this week?
2. Are people showing up? (show rate ≥ 80%)
3. Are people buying? (close rate ≥ 45%)

If #1 is off, stop — fix volume before anything else. If #1 is fine, move to #2. If #1 and #2 are fine, look at #3.
Monthly review (15 minutes)
At the end of each month:

1. Select the month in the dropdown and read the Recommendations section — it tells you exactly what to focus on next month.

2. Check the Source Performance table — which channels brought the most booked calls? Which had the best close rate? Double down on what's working.

3. Compare to last month — are numbers trending up or down?
When something looks off
The dashboard tells you where the problem is, not why. Use it as a starting point:

Low volume → Which channels slowed down? What was different in peak months?

High cancellations → Is there enough follow-up between booking and the call? Are leads qualified enough?

Low show rate → Are reminders being sent 24h and 1h before the call?

Low close rate → Is the sales call script being followed? Are objections being handled well?
How to read the Source Performance table
The table shows the last 6 months of data for each channel. Focus on two things:

Volume (Booked column) — green means the channel is active and bringing leads. Red means it needs attention.

Quality (Close Rate column) — a channel can bring lots of leads but if close rate is low, those leads aren't converting. The best channels have both high volume AND high close rate.
How to use the Weekly Breakdown table
The weekly table always shows the current month split into Mon–Sun weeks. Use it for your 5-minute weekly check-in.

Booked X/16: How many calls were scheduled this week vs the weekly target of 16. Green = on track, yellow = close, red = behind.

Showed X (Y%): How many people actually showed up, plus the show rate for that week. Color follows the same logic — green ≥ 80%, yellow 65–80%, red below 65%.

Sales X/5: How many sales closed this week vs the weekly target of ~4–5.

Status column: "✓ On track" means the week hit all three targets. "⚠ Close" means it was acceptable but not great. "✗ Below" means something needs attention. "● In progress" means the week is still running.

How to read it fast: scan the Status column first. If everything is green, you're good. If you see red or yellow, look at which metric caused it — Booked, Showed, or Sales — and that tells you exactly where to focus.
Keeping the data clean
This dashboard is only as good as the data in the Google Sheet. A few things to keep in mind:

Update the sheet consistently — make sure Call Status (showed / no show / cancelled) and End in Sale (yes/no) are filled in promptly after each call.

Mark refunds on the sale date — the Refund Status column should be updated as TRUE when a refund is processed, so it counts in the right month.

Refresh the dashboard after updating the sheet using the ↻ Refresh button in the top right.

Knowledge base — where each number comes from
Booked Calls — target ≥ 62/month
Counts all people who scheduled a sales call that month. In the current process, booking and applying happen at the same time — if someone applies, they already have a call scheduled.

Why 62: to close 17 sales you need 38 showed. Historically only 62% of booked calls actually show up (the rest cancel or no-show), so you need 62 booked to get 38 showed.
Calls Showed (Show Rate) — target ≥ 80%
Shows how many people who booked actually showed up to the call. Calculated as:

showed ÷ (showed + no-show)

Cancellations are not included because they are different — the person who cancels gave notice and can reschedule, the no-show simply didn't appear.

Why 80%: it's the real average from the last 14 months of data.
Cancellations — target ≤ 18%
Percentage of people who booked but cancelled before the call. Calculated as:

cancellations ÷ total booked

A cancellation is not necessarily a loss — there's an opportunity to reschedule. But if the % is high it means people weren't committed enough when booking, or follow-up was lacking.

Why 18%: real historical average from the last 14 months.
Sales (Close Rate) — target ≥ 45%
Shows how many people who showed up to the call ended up purchasing. Calculated as:

gross sales ÷ showed

This number measures sales team effectiveness on the call. If the % is low, the problem is not volume or follow-up — it's the call itself.

Why 45%: real historical average from the last 14 months.
Refunds — target ≤ 2/month
Counts sales that were refunded, using the sale date (not the refund date) so everything stays within the same month.

The historical average is 1.4 refunds/month. Having 1 or 2 is normal. The alert triggers at 3 or more — that indicates a pattern worth reviewing.

The worst month recorded was October 2025 with 5 refunds.
Net Sales — target 17/month
This is the final number that matters. Calculated as:

gross sales − refunds

Gross sales include everyone who purchased that month, even if they later requested a refund. Net sales is what was actually collected.

Why 17: it's the monthly goal set by the team. To get there you need the full funnel working: 62 booked → 38 showed → 17 sales.
